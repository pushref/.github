AIPushTech, what it is

AIPushTech is a new category at the intersection of three worlds nobody had seriously connected yet: artificial intelligence, technology watch, and continuous learning.

The idea fits in one sentence: AI comes to you, rather than the other way around. Instead of chasing every Twitter thread, every newsletter, every model release — AIPushTech sets up a system that filters, contextualizes, and confronts you with what truly matters in your field. It's active monitoring of AI applied to a profession, paired with a skill-building logic: you don't just consume, you grow.

What AIPushTech is not

  • Not an e-learning platform with linear modules and certifications to put on a CV.
  • Not a MOOC to consume once just to tick a box.
  • Not a school, not a course, not a fixed syllabus.

What AIPushTech is

  • A monitoring practice structured by professional domain — AI as it changes your work, not AI in general.
  • A learning thread that follows the actual release of tools, papers, and updates.
  • An intellectual discipline: understand, practice, retain.

Pushref, the product

What Pushref is not

  • Not an RSS aggregator that dumps a thousand feeds for you to sort yourself.
  • Not a boring online course that teaches AI in six chapters and a final quiz.
  • Not a search engine where you have to find your keywords manually, hope to land on the right page, and start over next week.

What Pushref is

  • An active and passive monitoring of AI applied to your domain. Active when you search; passive when you simply read the daily flow.
  • An editorial policy specific to each domain — its motto, its tone, its inclusion and exclusion criteria.
  • A system of probes — inclusive, exclusive, boost, strict_exclude — that score every article and decide whether it enters your flow.
  • A rigorous selection of sources, weighted by reliability and relevance to the domain.

The ambition is clear: to help you grow in your profession at the pace of AI, without becoming a full-time librarian. You read what matters. You practice in a sandbox. You retain via quizzes and spaced repetition. You share with peers.

Every time, a marketing narrative claiming everything would change, and a slower, more prosaic commercial reality. But AI, since 2024, is a real shift: his CIO clients no longer ask "if" but "how and when," and the salesperson who can't answer concretely loses meetings to the Big 4 or AI pure players.

Timeline — six milestones that moved IT services sales

  • NOVEMBER 2022 ChatGPT lands at the CIOs First meetings where the CIO asks "and AI on your side?" without knowing what to expect. Evangelization phase: more talking than selling.
  • MID-2023 POCs everywhere, deployments rare Experimental AI budgets unlock. ITSF firms that know how to sell a fixed-price POC (€80-120k, 8 weeks) gain the edge. Learning phase.
  • EARLY 2024 Microsoft Copilot E5 generalized First real wave of enterprise AI purchases. Microsoft sales reps short-circuit ITSFs on their own clients. First major alarm on the need to upgrade.
  • LATE 2024 Use-case maturity Clients stop asking for "some AI" and start asking for targeted copilots, agents, RAG. ITSFs that trained their sales win. Those still chasing vague briefs lose.
  • MID-2025 Agentic in production · pricing evolves First real agents in production at major accounts. Tickets move from €80k (POC) to €300-800k (integration). But failure risk also rises.
  • 2026 AI Act applies · compliance consulting August 2, 2026 deadline for the main obligations. ITSFs that know how to sell compliance consulting (and not just build) open a new revenue line.

Tangible impacts today

  • The client brief becomes the topic, not the project. Real sales work is no longer proposing a schedule, it's transforming a fuzzy AI demand into a realistic spec. Rare skill, valued.
  • Competition reconfigures violently. AI pure players (specialized startups) on depth, Big 4 on strategy, GAFAM consulting on obviousness. The classic ITSF must pick an angle.
  • Sales reps who train widen the gap. No need to become an engineer — but knowing how to distinguish an agent from RAG, fine-tuning from prompting, a POC from an MVP, wins deals.
  • Pre-sales engineering becomes strategic. The salesperson + pre-sales pair becomes a differentiator. ITSFs without AI-trained pre-sales lose pitches at the first pointed question.

Pushref mechanics for the commercial-esn domain

Pushref doesn't read the web for you. It reads the web for your domain. The difference lies in a precise mechanism: probes. A probe is a keyword loaded with editorial intent; it says "this article is for this domain" or "this article is not for this domain." Pushref applies these probes to every ingested article, computes a score, and decides whether the article enters your flow.

[ HOW IT WORKS ]

Four probe types come into play: inclusive (weight +1.0, the article must touch one), boost (weight +1.5, raises the score without being required), exclusive (weight −1.0, penalizes to likely ejection), strict_exclude (weight −10.0, ejection without appeal). Probes also feed the system prompts for enrichment, quizzes, and sandbox — they frame the domain's tone and scope beyond mere filtering.

On commercial-esn, probes are calibrated on immediate sales utility: qualify a need, scope a POC, distinguish vendor tools, sell compliance, parry a technical question in a meeting. They exclude code and implementation (which belong to the developer domain), toxic prospecting hacks, and hype without argumentative value.

Probe zoom — the seven pivots

qualification-discovery
Qualify poorly formulated AI demands. MEDDIC/MEDDPICC adapted to AI. Transform "we want to do AI" into a realistic spec.
use-cases-pricing
Enterprise use cases (copilots, agents, corporate RAG), sizing, POC pricing, measured ROI. The heart of the sales conversation.
enterprise-vendors
Microsoft 365 Copilot, Claude Enterprise, Gemini Enterprise, Mistral, AWS Bedrock, Salesforce Einstein, ServiceNow AI. The solutions panorama to compare.
technical-concepts
RAG, agent, MCP, fine-tuning vs prompting, context window, hallucination — vulgarized to sell, not to code.
regulation-compliance
AI Act, GDPR-AI, governance, internal charter, processing register. The new consulting line to sell.
sales-ecosystem
Pitches, RFPs, vendor partnerships, Big 4 competition, AI pure players. The commercial ecosystem you operate in.
security-shadow-ai
Shadow AI, prompt injection, prompt leaks, data classification, ISO 42001. Topic rising in security pre-sales.

And conversely — what probes exclude

Exclusive probes protect the flow from what would waste Mehdi's time: 5 magic prompts to sell more, 10 AI sales hacks, passive income sales, MLM sales, toxic cold call automation, arXiv paper, transformer architecture, SWE-bench. Anything that is hustle, sales manipulation, or pure technical (useful to the developer domain, not to sales) stays out.

The Pushref journey, step by step

The Pushref journey isn't linear. It's a cycle, and any user can enter at any point, leave, come back. But there's a recommended order that maximizes learning.

PUSHREF JOURNEY · DOMAIN COMMERCIAL-ESN

1. The level test

Twenty-five questions spanning all probes of the domain — not a sample, not a shortcut: every theme Pushref ingests for commercial-esn is represented. Three possible outcomes: intermediate, advanced, expert. Your level adjusts analysis depth, quiz calibration, and the complexity of suggested sandbox prompts.

2. The flow of enriched articles

Each article has been pre-enriched by an LLM using the domain's system prompt: context, summary with numbers preserved, key takeaways, actionable argument for a meeting. The tone is calibrated for commercial-esn: pragmatic, honest, field-tested — an experienced salesperson talking to an experienced salesperson.

3. Quizzes and SRS reviews

For each opened article, an auto-generated quiz offers 4 to 6 questions — on tools, numbers, methodologies, traps. Wrong answers enter a spaced repetition queue à la SuperMemo. A single weekly reminder if the queue exceeds ten pending items.

4. The cross-LLM comparison sandbox

You ask a question — for example "write me a response to an AI RFP for a 200-store retailer, with sizing, schedule, and commercial terms" — and Pushref shows you side by side the answers from Claude, GPT, Gemini, and Mistral. You compare rigor, tone, sales relevance. Several sandboxes are planned; for now, one is available — the most structuring: the cross-LLM sandbox.

5. Sharing

You can share an enriched article, a sandbox run, a quiz result, or your personal weekly note. Each link is public, stamped with your handle and avatar — with a dynamic OG image for LinkedIn, sales team, or pre-sales engineering.
